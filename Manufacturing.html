Manufacturing Automation

Upgraded manufacturing process for lights-out operation and minimal waste

Skills Involved

  • 6 DoF robotic arms
  • Industrial wiring
  • PLC programming
  • Pneumatics
  • Manufacturing process development

Objectives:

Our objective was to demonstrate to an automotive engineering partner the effectiveness of our prototyped automation upgrades for their manual manufacturing processes. As a collaborative effort among four members, our MEng project involved analysing the partner's current manufacturing process and designing, prototyping, and benchmarking an automated replacement. In addition to this primary goal, we aimed to showcase the advantages of automation to the partner and provide a blueprint for future manufacturing upgrades. The manufacturing process we focused on involved depalletizing metal parts, applying liquid sealant and a rubber seal, and ejecting the finished part onto a conveyor.

Techincal:

To meet the partner's design specifications for cycle time, safety, and complexity, we collectively developed process concept designs, which were then reviewed and approved by the partner. As per their request, we integrated their existing manual sealant application machine into the workcell design. For depalletizing and seal application tasks, we selected Universal Robots UR10e and UR3e, respectively, and upgraded the manual machine to interface seamlessly with these robotic arms. My responsibilities within the project encompassed electrical analysis, design, and robotic arm programming. Initially, I thoroughly analysed the electrical systems of the manual process and subsequently reengineered them to ensure compatibility with Universal Robots' UR10e Cobot arm. To maintain consistency with the original machine's documentation, I meticulously created electrical schematics using AutoCAD Electrical. Additionally, I developed a replacement PLC program for the manual machine, enabling it to be triggered from the cobot's controller instead of relying on its human interface. Utilizing Polyscope, I programmed all Cobots, starting with the depalletizing operation. This involved implementing four box-pick operations to remove parts identified with a 2.5D vision system, followed by the removal of the box to a spare pallet. We trained the vision system to accommodate variations in part placement and thoroughly assessed vision performance under different lighting conditions to simulate the factory environment. Subsequently, we programmed the rubber seal application, leveraging computer vision to detect parts and determine optimal placement locations, while also accounting for placement variability. Finally, we conducted comprehensive analysis of process cycle times at varying joint speeds and accelerations to meet the desired specifications.

Outcomes:

Collectively, our team successfully validated each aspect of the work cell in isolation, including depalletizing, seal application, and sealant application. We substantiated the benefits of automation to the partner by presenting them with cycle time data and return on investment calculations. However, due to COVID-related supply chain restrictions, we faced delays in procuring the Cobots, which impeded full-scale testing and deployment of the complete cell within the allotted timeframe of our degrees. Nevertheless, we delivered comprehensive designs to the partner, which were utilized by on-site engineers to complete the project's deployment.
