Warehouse Management:

In collaboration with government research partners, I am currently engaged in an ongoing research project focused on UAVs for warehouse management. This requires creation of a bespoke UAV platform, a customised mobile localisation system, UAV-UGV robot collaboration through ROS/ROS2, and 3D LiDAR SLAM. This project has not only expanded my knowledge of ROS and network topologies but has also provided valuable experience in custom UAV design and agile development methodologies with sprints. It has been a significant milestone in my professional growth, bridging the gap between low-level embedded systems programming and high-level robotics.

Monocopter Image Capture:

Another intriguing project I have been involved in is centred around the enhancement of monocopters, also known as samara autorotating wings, for image capture purposes. These unique craft, due to their high-frequency rotation, present challenges in capturing high-quality images, as distortion and artifacts often arise. Additionally, keeping the weight of additional sensors to a minimum is crucial for the proper functioning of these lightweight craft. Therefore, our project has primarily focused on meticulous camera hardware selection, fine-tuning camera parameters, and software optimization to enable precise image captures. Moreover, we are aiming to transmit these images in real-time to a remote operator as a video feed, which requires extensive research into embedded systems image capture and manipulation. This endeavour has allowed me to delve deep into the realm of image processing and has been an enriching experience in understanding the intricacies of optimizing embedded systems to achieve the desired results.
